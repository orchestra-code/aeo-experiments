# Companion blog post brief — <experiment slug>

The research article carries the methods; the blog post carries the
*implications*. Different audience, different job.

- **Where:** main spyglasses repo, `apps/web/content/posts/<slug>.mdx` **and**
  `<slug>.de.mdx` (both required; no date prefix in the filename).
- **Frontmatter:** copy from a recent post — `title, date, image, authorName,
  authorImage, authorLinkedIn, excerpt, tags, published, faq[]`.
- **Audience:** marketing and PR firms managing brand visibility in AI
  answers. Lead with what they should DO differently, not how we modelled it.
- **Canonical:** link to the research article on research.spyglasses.io as the
  source ("full methodology and data"); don't restate the stats machinery.
- **Tone/positioning:** use the `marketing-strategy` skill when drafting.

## Structure that works

1. The finding in one sentence, framed as client advice.
2. Why the intuitive alternative is wrong (one paragraph, no math).
3. What to change in strategy — concrete, this-quarter actions.
4. The lead chart (watermarked figure from the study).
5. One honest limitation ("what this doesn't mean") — credibility beats hype.
6. CTA: run your own visibility report on Spyglasses.

## House rules (same as the article)

- Sample sizes phrased as "citations evaluated in this study".
- No customer prompts/responses/fan-out text, even as examples.
- Claims must not exceed the article's "What we can and cannot claim" section.
